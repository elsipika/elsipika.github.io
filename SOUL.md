# SOUL.md — The Spirit of This Site

## Who this is for

Selina Sun: software engineer, QA engineer, CS graduate, artist, gamer, bunny-lover. The site should feel like her — technically capable, a little whimsical, not corporate.

## Aesthetic DNA

**Lavender and pixel art.** The purple/lavender palette and VT323 pixel font are intentional and personal — they echo game aesthetics, retro computing, and a distinctly soft creative sensibility. Don't swap them for something "more professional." This palette is the brand.

**Warmth over polish.** The Skitty GIF, the gameboy GIF, the lowercase section headings — these exist on purpose. They signal that Selina is a real human being who brings enthusiasm and personality to her work, not a resume in a box.

**Charm without clutter.** Each section has one focal GIF and concise content. Resist adding more imagery, animations, or decorative elements. The card-based, narrow-column layout creates intimacy and keeps focus.

## Voice

- Lowercase, minimal punctuation in headings: `software engineer · qa · creative`
- Friendly, confident, not boastful in bio copy
- Let skills speak for themselves in lists — no adjectives like "expert" or "proficient"
- Footer sign-off: `let's connect.` — warm, open, short

## What this site is not

- Not a slide deck. Not a list of buzzwords. Not a tech bro portfolio.
- Not dark mode industrial (lots of portfolios go this direction — this one doesn't)
- Not a wall of projects. Three curated projects with real descriptions beat twelve thumbnail links.

## Color meaning

| Color | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| `#E5BEFA` light lavender | Warmth, creativity, approachability |
| `#bc8cd6` medium purple | Technical depth, confidence |
| `#caabdf` card bg | Calm professionalism |
| White text | Clarity, cleanliness |

## What to preserve in future edits

1. The lavender background photo
2. The VT323 pixel font for all display headings
3. The three GIFs (skitty, gameboy, laptop) — they're personality, not decoration
4. The lowercase `.section-heading` style
5. The single-card centered layout — resist the urge to go full-page or multi-column
6. The color palette (no grays, no whites, no darks as primary colors)
